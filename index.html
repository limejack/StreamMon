ABOUT STREAM MONITORING

Stream monitors serve an incredibly important role in our community. They monitor the water quality of our streams so we can identify unhealthy streams which can be unsafe for swimming, fishing, playing in, and drinking. This information is gathered from volunteers throughout the country. Monitors collect and identify benthic macroinvertebrates (such as crawfish, leeches, dragonfly larvae, etc.) and, from that information, can service a stream health score on a scale of 1-12. In Virginia, Virginia Save Our Streams is a program run by the Izaak Walton League of America which is a national level conservation organization and works to improve water quality across the country.

This website aims to facilitate the process by running the necessary calculations for finding the stream health score based on information inputted by the user about the number of each type of benthic macroinvertebrate found.
